Common questions

Everything you
want to know

Our database is cross-referenced against peer-reviewed studies, the FDA's GRAS list, EU food safety regulations, and WHO guidelines. We achieve 98% accuracy on labeled products and flag ambiguous cases clearly rather than guessing.
Yes. NutraLens covers food, beverages, dietary supplements, skincare, personal care, and household cleaning products. The ingredient database spans 180,000+ compounds across all categories, including INCI names used in cosmetics.
Your scan history is stored locally on your device by default. If you opt into cloud sync, data is encrypted end-to-end and never sold or shared with third parties. We are SOC 2 Type II certified and GDPR compliant.
NutraLens can analyze ingredient lists directly from the label using OCR — no barcode needed. If a product is new to our database, your scan contributes to building it. We add approximately 4,000 new products per week.
The free plan includes unlimited scans, full ingredient breakdowns, health scores, and basic alternative recommendations. NutraLens Pro adds exposure tracking, personalized profiles, detailed regulatory comparisons, and PDF report exports.
Scores run 0–100 and weight ingredients by risk level, volume, and regulatory status. A score above 75 is generally considered low-risk. Scores factor in your personal profile if set — the same product may score differently for someone with a nut allergy versus someone without.
